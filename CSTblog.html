First Days Term Two

January 5, 2015 by Armin

Well term two has begun. It started yesterday and although the labs are mostly cancelled this week, we still have lectures all week with the occassional lab. This is nice but also can create problems depending on your schedule. Our set/cohort, Set D, almost got stuck with a 5 hour break between morning lab and afternoon lectures. Luckily, our morning instructor moved things to another day so that was great. I've met all of my lecture instructors now, with only a couple of lab instructors to meet.

Teachers

To summarize, so far so good. Most of the teachers seem really great so far. For Discrete Math we have a man called Goran Ruzic and he seems really cool. Like the name suggests, he seems to be Eastern European of some kind. He speaks clearly and is very deliberate. Teaching in general seems different this term, less power point style teaching and more hands on example type teachin and Goran follows this trend.

For Java level 2 we have a man called Dennis and he is very down to earth. He comes off as being just like one of us. He seems like a coder and seems very relateable. I'm looking forward to that class as he discussed we will be building bigger programs some of it being over time and slowly adding modules to the programs. That sounds very interesting.

We have a man called Albert H. Wei for our C programming course. I have been told in the past by other T.A.'s and teachers that Albert is an absolute genius. With my first impressions under my belt, I will say that he is very unique to say the least. He is an older Asian gentleman and has a very comical way of talking, but he is able to keep people's attention and make them laugh too. Apparently he is very straight forward and tells you what's on the exams and what to do for them. C programming is a lot harder than Java, or so i've heard, so i'm interested to see how this turns out. Really looking forward to his course.

Computer Architecture is another course we have and the teacher is Jason Harrison. I've taken a couple courses with Jason already and I think he is absolutely great. I've gotten used to his straight forward tell-it-like-it-is method of teaching. He doesn't waste time, he's right to the point and no B.S. Students have already seem to have taken to him just like i took to him in my previous courses. He told us how to be successful in the course and more importantly gave us a big warning in that if we fall behind in the course we will probably not be able to catch up. It'll be interesting to see the differences between how he teaches this course and other courses.

I had my first Database lecture today by Keith Tang. Man, i hate to be that guy but holy that was boring. It's a two hour lecture, which is rare, but this is gonna be painful. Part of the reason that's going to be the case is because it is a two hour lecture on one of the more boring topics after a REALY long day. I do like him however, as he is very good at explaining things and making sure we understand them so far. Keith is definitely an old school type of guy, but i dont mind that, for me it's more the lateness of the course and the length of the lecture. It's always hard to keep students entertained for 2 hours in a lecture but doing it when you are teaching database has got to be hard. We'll see how this course pans out, but databases are an integral part of any software dev's tool kit.

The last course is a communications course and the teacher is the same as last term and if you have read my december term one summary blogs then you will know that it is Frank Schnurr. I'm not looking forward to communications however, Frank is great and im looking forward to interacting with him again.

Finally

Well so far i've enjoyed meeting my teachers and seeing my term one friends again. I'm really going to try and keep this blog going throughout the year, it's one of my goals. However, I don't expect to do it more than once or twice a week as things get going. Anyways, i look forward to what this term brings and hopefully i pass, as this is one of the terms of death. Thanks
